The Model 1 Agreement (14 Pages)&lt;ref&gt;https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/tax-policy/treaties/Documents/FATCA-Agreement-BVI-6-30-2014.pdf&lt;/ref&gt; recognizes that: ''The Government of Great Britain and Northern Ireland provided a copy of the Letter of Entrustment which was sent to the Government of the British Virgin Islands, to the Government of the United States of America &quot;via diplomatic note of May 28, 2014&quot;.''

The Letter of Entrustment dated July 14, 2010 was originally provided to the Government of the British Virgin Islands and authorised the Government of the BVI &quot;to negotiate and conclude Agreements relating to taxation that provide for exchange of information on tax matters to the OECD standard&quot; (Paragraph 2 of the FATCA Agreement). Via an &quot;Entrustment Letter&quot; dated March 24, 2014, The Govt of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, authorized the Govt of the BVI to sign an agreement on information exchange to facilitate the Implementation of the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act.&lt;ref&gt;https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/tax-policy/treaties/Documents/FATCA-Agreement-BVI-6-30-2014.pdf.&lt;/ref&gt; On March 27, 2017, the US Treasury site disclosed that the Model 1 agreement and related agreement were &quot;In Force&quot; on July 13, 2015.

====Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act====
Under the UK Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2018, beneficial ownership of companies in British overseas territories such as the British Virgin Islands must be publicly registered for disclosure by 31 December 2020.&lt;ref name=kmppvap&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.lawgazette.co.uk/law/overseas-territories-to-fight-public-register-demands/5066370.article|title=Overseas territories to fight public register demands|publisher=}}&lt;/ref&gt;  The Government of the British Virgin Islands plans to challenge this law, arguing that it violates the Constitutional sovereignty granted to the islands.&lt;ref name=kmppvap /&gt;

===Agriculture and industry===
Agriculture and industry account for only a small proportion of the islands' GDP.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/vi.html |title=CIA World Factbook- British Virgin Islands|accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt; Agricultural produce includes fruit, vegetables, sugar cane, livestock and poultry, and industries include rum distillation, construction and boat building. Commercial fishing is also practised in the islands' waters.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/British-Virgin-Islands |title=Encyclopedia Britannica - BVI |accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt;

===Workforce===
The British Virgin Islands is heavily dependent on migrant workers, and over 50% of all workers on the islands are of a foreign descent. Only 37% of the entire population were born in the territory.&lt;ref name=&quot;ReferenceB&quot;/&gt; The national labour-force is estimated at 12,770, of whom approximately 59.4% work in the service sector but less than 0.6% are estimated to work in agriculture (the balance working in industry).&lt;ref name=&quot;The Times&quot;&gt;{{cite web|url=http://bcg.thetimes.co.uk/North_America/British_Virgin_Islands#workforce|title=British Virgin Islands – workforce|author=''[[The Times]]''|accessdate=2012-03-19}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/vi.html |title=CIA World Factbook- British Virgin Islands|accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt;

====CARICOM status &amp; The CARICOM Single Market Economy====
According to the membership section of the CARICOM Community site, as of July 2, 1991,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://caricom.org/about-caricom/who-we-are/our-governance/heads-of-government/british-virgin-islands|title=Country Profile for British Virgin Islands —Caribbean Community (CARICOM) Secretariat|first=CARICOM|last=Secretariat|website=caricom.org}}&lt;/ref&gt; The British Virgin Islands holds Associate Member&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://caricom.org/|title=Caribbean Community (CARICOM) —Caribbean Community (CARICOM) Secretariat|first=CARICOM|last=Secretariat|website=caricom.org}}&lt;/ref&gt; status in CARICOM.

In recognition of the CARICOM (Free Movement) Skilled Persons Act which came into effect in July 1997 in some of the CARICOM countries such as Jamaica and which has been adopted in other CARICOM countries,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mlss.gov.jm/pub/index.php?artid=43|title=: Caribbean Single Market Economy (CSME) :.:: Ministry of Labour and Social Security ::.:|website=www.mlss.gov.jm}}&lt;/ref&gt; such as Trinidad and Tobago,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://immigration.gov.tt/Services/CSME.aspx|title=CSME|first=Immigration Division Ministry of National|last=Security|website=immigration.gov.tt|access-date=25 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170326135453/http://immigration.gov.tt/Services/CSME.aspx|archive-date=26 March 2017|url-status=dead}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;http://www.caribbeanjobs.com/careers/page/How-to-Apply-for-a-CARICOM-Skills-Certificate.aspx&lt;/ref&gt; it is possible that CARICOM nationals who hold the &quot;A Certificate of Recognition of Caribbean Community Skilled Person&quot; may be allowed to work in the BVI under normal working conditions.

==Transport==
{{Main|Transport in the British Virgin Islands}}
[[File:Tortola-airport.jpg|thumb|right|Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport on Tortola]]
There are {{convert|113|km|mi|0}} of roads. The main airport, [[Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport]], also known as Beef Island Airport, is located on Beef Island, which lies off the eastern tip of Tortola and is accessible by the [[Queen Elizabeth II Bridge, British Virgin Islands|Queen Elizabeth II Bridge]]. [[Cape Air]], [[LIAT]] and [[Air Sunshine]] are among the airlines offering scheduled service.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite web|url=http://www.bviaa.com/airlines|title=Airlines-BVI Airport|website=www.bviaa.com|access-date=2019-09-20}}&lt;/ref&gt; [[Virgin Gorda Airport|Virgin Gorda]] and [[Auguste George Airport|Anegada]] have their own smaller airports. Private air charter services operated by [[Island Birds]] Air Charter fly directly to all three islands from any major airport in the Caribbean.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite web|url=https://www.islandbirds.com/destinations/|title=Private Air Charter throughout the entire Caribbean.|website=Island Birds|language=en-US|access-date=2019-09-20}}&lt;/ref&gt; Helicopters are used to get to islands with no runway facilities; Antilles Helicopter Services is the only helicopter service based in the country.

The main harbour is in [[Road Town]]. There are also ferries that operate within the British Virgin Islands and to the neighbouring United States Virgin Islands. As in the UK and in the United States Virgin Islands, cars in the British Virgin Islands [[Right- and left-hand traffic#Jurisdictions with left-hand traffic|drive on the left]]; however, nearly all cars are [[left hand drive]],&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.fco.gov.uk/en/travel-and-living-abroad/travel-advice-by-country/north-central-america/british-virgin-islands/|title=British Virgin Islands (British Overseas Territory) travel advice - GOV.UK|work=fco.gov.uk}}&lt;/ref&gt; being imported from the United States. The roads are often quite steep, narrow and winding, and ruts can be a problem when it rains.

==Demographics==
{{Main|Demographics of the British Virgin Islands}}
As of the 2010 Census, the population of the territory was 28,054.&lt;ref name=&quot;ReferenceB&quot;/&gt; Current estimates put the population at 35,800 (July 2018).&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/vi.html |title=CIA World Factbook- British Virgin Islands|accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt; The majority of the population (83%) are [[wikt:Afro-Caribbean|Afro-Caribbean]], descended from slaves brought to the islands by the British.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/British-Virgin-Islands |title=Encyclopedia Britannica - BVI |accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt; Other large ethnic groups include Latinos (5.5%), those of European ancestry (5.4%), native Amerindian (2.1%) and [[India|Indian]] (1.6%).&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/vi.html |title=CIA World Factbook- British Virgin Islands|accessdate= 13 July 2019}}&lt;/ref&gt;

The 2004 Census reports:
* 83.4% [[Afro-Caribbean|African]]
* 7% European/Caucasian
* 9.6% Others*
{{Smaller|* Includes [[Indo-Caribbean|Indian]], Carib/Amerindian, Black/Carib mixed, and mixed-race Hispanic}}

The 2010 Census reports the main places of origin of residents as follows:&lt;ref name=&quot;ReferenceB&quot;/&gt;
* 37% local born (though note that many locals go to St. Thomas or the United States for maternity services){{citation needed|date=July 2019}}
* 7.2% Guyana
* 7.0% St. Vincent and the Grenadines
* 6.0% Jamaica
* 5.5% United States
* 5.4% Dominican Republic
* 5.3% United States Virgin Islands
{{Smaller|* Includes [[Indo-Caribbean|Indian]], Carib/Amerindian, Black/Carib mixed, and mixed-race Hispanic.}}

The islands are heavily dependent upon migrant labour. In 2004, migrant workers accounted for 50% of the total population.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}} 32% of workers employed in the British Virgin Islands work for the government.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}}

Unusually, the territory has one of the highest drowning mortality rates in the world, being higher than other high risk countries such as China and India.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://c.ymcdn.com/sites/www.usla.org/resource/resmgr/lifeguard_library/drowningfactsheetwhosep03.pdf|title=&quot;Drowning&quot;, publication World Health Organization|publisher=}}&lt;/ref&gt; 20% of deaths in the British Virgin Islands during 2012 were recorded as drownings,&lt;ref name=&quot;ReferenceA&quot;&gt;The BVI Beacon, Thursday, August 15, 2013 article entitled &quot;Report: Passports up, marriages down last year&quot;.&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;Annual Report of the Civil Registry and Passport Office for 2012 which includes &quot;For the 20 percent that represented drowning, all were tourists who died from snorkelling or diving in the VI waters in and around caves at [[Norman Island]], as well as near [[Virgin Gorda]]...The Virgin Islands should, therefore put safety measures in place such as the dissemination of information to hotels, dive shops and marinas.&quot; The same report confirms that the deaths of 86 persons were recorded in the Territory during 2012.&lt;/ref&gt; all of them being tourists. Despite this, the territory's most popular beach still has no lifeguard presence.&lt;ref name=&quot;ReferenceA&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.virginislandsnewsonline.com/en/news/no-lifeguards-at-cane-garden-bay-virgin-gorda-beaches|title=No lifeguards at Cane Garden Bay &amp; Virgin Gorda beaches|work=virginislandsnewsonline.com}}&lt;/ref&gt;
